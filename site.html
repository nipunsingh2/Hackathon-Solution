India's Rich Cultural Heritage and Tradition

Indian word for culture is ‘Sanskriti’. From time immemorial, Indians have described their culture as ‘Human Culture’ (Manav Dharma/ Sanskriti). It has a universal appeal. The principle of ‘Unity in Diversity’ is the implicit law of nature, universe and life. People belonging to diverse communities, speaking different languages, having different food, performing different customs harmoniously co-exist in India. Thus, the soul of cultural heritage of India lies in the fact that it’s an all embracing confluence of religions, traditions, customs and beliefs.



Over the years, numerous styles of art, architecture, painting, music, dance, festivals and customs have developed in India and this wide variety has made the Indian culture unparalled to which the entire world still looks up to. The cultural heritage of India still flourishes maintaining its original features together along with changes,a key indicator of its strength

Indian Art

Indian art is a reflection of the country's cultural diversity, with a rich history that dates back to ancient times. From cave paintings to modern-day sculptures, Indian art has evolved over the centuries, reflecting the changing socio-political landscape of the country.

Indian Festivals

India is known for its colorful festivals, which are celebrated with enthusiasm and fervor across the country. From Diwali to Holi, Durga Puja to Pongal, each festival has its unique significance and cultural importance.

Indian Music

Indian music is an integral part of the country's cultural heritage, with a rich history that dates back to ancient times. From classical music to modern-day Bollywood songs, Indian music is diverse and vibrant, reflecting the country's cultural diversity.

Indian Dance

Indian dance is a beautiful and expressive art form that has evolved over the centuries. From classical dance styles like Bharatanatyam and Kathak to modern-day Bollywood dance, Indian dance is a reflection of the country's cultural diversity.

Indian Food

Indian food is a gastronomic delight, with a diverse range of flavors, textures, and ingredients. From spicy curries to sweet desserts, Indian food is a reflection of the country's cultural diversity, with each region having its unique culinary traditions.
